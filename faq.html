  1. What is Lacrosse?

    Lacrosse is a sport similart to hockey or field hockey. It is played with sticks with mesh pockets. The object of the sport is to score on a six foot by six foot goal. Team with the most goals after a certain amount of time wins.

  2. How is Men's Lacrosse played?

    Lacrosse is played with 10 players at a time on each team. There are nine field players and one goalie on each team. Only six players can play offense or defense at one time. Their are four positions Attack, Midfield, Defense, and Goalie.

  3. What equipment do you need to play Men's Lacrosse?

    In order to play Lacrosse a player needs a Lacrosse Stick which includes a shaft and a head. For safety a player will need a helment, gloves, elbow pads, shoulder pads, and rib pads. There is optional protection for the legs but most players do not wear them. If you have further questions about equipment please see the video below.

  4. Is there a difference between Men's and Women's Lacrosse?

    Yes there is a difference. Men's lacrosse is a more physical game while Women's lacrosse has more finesse. Men's and Women's lacrosse have different rule sets but both games have the same general concept.

  5. What is the history of Lacrosse?

    Lacrosse is a sport that was created by Native Americans. The Native Americans still practice the original game to this day. The original game was played by hundreds at a time. It was used as a ritual game. The goals were generally 500 yards apart and rules where decided on the day of the game.
